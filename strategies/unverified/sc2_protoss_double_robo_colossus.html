Double-Robo into Colossus: a 2-base timing that needs one fix

A two-base timing build for a low-micro player. Two Gateways go down at 1:00 and 1:35, a quick Nexus follows around 2:10-3:10, and a double Robotics Facility turns the mid-game into a wall of Colossi. The replays show one flaw: the robo starts too late, so detection arrives after the Dark Templars. This page shows the gap and the fix.

Protoss 2v2 low-micro patch 5.0.16

Two gates, then a quick expand

Gateway at 1:00, Gateway #2 at 1:35, Nexus around 2:10-3:10. The cleanest wins expanded near 2:09. This is a two-base timing, not an open-ended macro game.

The robo is too late

In the replays the robo starts ~4:00-4:15, so the Observer only pops 5:20, after the 5:00 DT window. That late detection is the single reason games were lost.

Colossi are the win army

First Immortal ~6:30, first Colossus ~7:26, then a second Facility and a Bay crank Colossi from both robos. That part already works.

Dante's standing rule

Two-base timing every game: Pylon, two Gateways at 1:00 and 1:35, Cyber Core, then a quick Nexus. Build Stalkers for early defense while you take the second base and tech. First robo unit is always an Observer, then an Immortal.

Why double robo

  • Observer gives permanent detection, but only if the robo starts on time. Start it by 3:15-3:30.
  • Immortal is a defender you point and click, no micro tax.
  • Two robos + Bay means Colossi arrive fast enough to matter and keep coming.

Resource note

These are replay-observed start landmarks, not resource-gated frames. If a structure is not affordable at its slot, finish the current probe pump first. Banking 500+ minerals means you cut too hard.

The one thing that loses the game

Danger, your Observer is late

Observer at 5:20 today, the target is 4:34

Across the replays the robo starts 4:00-4:15, so the Observer only pops 5:20, after the 5:00 Dark Templar window. That is very likely why the DT rush got through. The whole gap is the robo start time.

The fix: put the Robotics Facility down by 3:15-3:30, about 45 seconds sooner than the current habit. Then the Observer pops 4:34-4:45, before the DTs land, and the Immortal and Stalkers do the killing once they are lit up. The greedier wins already did this: Rainfall expanded at 2:09 and had detection by 4:57.

Against the DT rush

Two things beat a Dark Templar rush: detection by ~4:30 and something that shoots by 5:00. With the current robo timing you get neither in time, the Observer only lands 5:20, so pull the robo earlier as above. In 2v2, a partner Terran Scan is free instant detection, so lean on it while your own Observer is still late or if it dies. Do not chase the DT player's workers, his economy is already spent on the tech, just hold and out-macro him.
